# Content Brief: Why an AI Knowledge Base Needs a Raw Source Layer

## Topic

Many people use AI to organize materials and only keep polished summaries, while losing the original evidence. A long-term AI knowledge base should preserve sources first, then generate research notes and wiki pages.

## Audience

- Obsidian knowledge management users.
- People using Claude Code, Codex, or Gemini for source processing.
- Technical practitioners and creators building personal AI workflows.

## Core claim

The key to an AI knowledge base is not how fast it summarizes. The key is whether you can later trace where each conclusion came from.

## Structure

1. Hook: the more AI summaries you create, the easier it is to lose the evidence chain.
2. Problem: many knowledge bases store conclusions but not sources.
3. Judgment: the raw source layer is the foundation of a long-term knowledge system.
4. Example: source card → research note → wiki page.
5. Closing prompt: can your current knowledge base trace the source of each judgment?

Many people start an AI knowledge base by asking the model to summarize, classify, and write notes.
